About Piezoelectric TILE

Piezoelectric tiles are a type of flooring that can generate electricity from footsteps. They are made using special materials, such as crystals and ceramics, in which electric charge builds up when mechanical stress is applied. The energy generated through piezoelectric tiles can be used to power household appliances, streetlights, and other devices. Piezoelectric tiles are budget-friendly and can be installed in public or commercial areas with heavy footfall.

How To Works it

Piezoelectric tiles work by converting mechanical energy into electrical energy. When a person steps on a piezoelectric tile, they apply pressure on the material, which causes electric charge to accumulate on the surface. This charge can then be collected and used to power devices. The more people walk on the tiles, the more electricity is generated. Piezoelectric tiles are made of materials that have the piezoelectric effect, such as crystals and ceramics. These materials have a special structure that allows them to change shape when an electric field is applied or vice versa. Piezoelectric tiles can be installed in places where there is a lot of foot traffic, such as airports, malls, stadiums, or sidewalks. They can reduce the dependence on fossil fuels and provide a clean and renewable source of energy.
